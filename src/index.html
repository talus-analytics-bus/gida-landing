The GEORGETOWN INFECTIOUS DISEASE ATLAS is an integrated research platform providing quick access to a comprehensive set of global health security visualization dashboards, decision support tools, and data libraries developed by the Center for Global Health Science and Security at Georgetown University. See below for a description of each tool, links to the sites, and access to a quick download of the data from each.

WEBSITES AND TOOLS
GLOBAL HEALTH SECURITY TRACKING
GHSAURL.COM

The Global Health Security Tracking site is an integrated mapping and data visualization platform showing the flow of funding for global health security from funder to recipient globally. Funds are categorized by their intended impact on improving health security, including funds targeted for specific outbreak response efforts.

Key Citation: Katz, R., E. Graeden, J. Kerr, S. Eaneff (2019). Tracking the flow of funds in global health security. EcoHealth. 1-8.
GEORGETOWN OUTBREAK ACTIVITY LIBRARY
GHSAURL.COM

The Georgetown Outbreak Activity Library provides rapid access and search for a comprehensive library of the activities required for response to a large-scale international outbreak, including case studies, and information about the stakeholders and policies mapped to each activity.

GEORGETOWN BIOSCENARIOS
GHSAURL.COM

Bioscenarios is a rapid visualization tool demonstrating the diversity in biological outbreak scenarios based on the requirements for responding to the different types of events. Each event is coded by 12 characteristics and ranked by relative likelihood to cause fatalities in humans and animals.

Key citation: Katz, R., E. Graeden, J. Kerr (2018). The complexity of biological events. The Lancet Global Health. 6-2: 136-137.
DELIBERATE BIOLOGICAL SCENARIOS
GHSAURL.COM

Deliberate Biological Scenarios is an interactive teaching tool that aligns policies with the stakeholders mandated or authorized to act in responding to a deliberate biological outbreak (e.g., bioterror or state-sponsored attack). Each policy is associated with when during the outbreak it is triggered, providing an analysis platform to identify policy gaps and define roles.

Key citation: Katz, R., E. Graeden, K. Abe, A. Attal-Juncqua, M. Boyce, S. Eaneff (2018). Mapping stakeholder and policies in response to deliberate biological events. Heliyon. 4(12): e01091
